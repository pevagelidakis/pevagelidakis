## 🔬 Research Focus

### Time-Series & Statistical Synthesis
My MSc thesis — awarded **Best Thesis 2025** within the DAMSL programme and selected for dissemination at the **Greeks in AI Symposium (Athens, 2025)** — develops robust penalised estimators for periodic, non-stationary time series. This work has direct methodological overlap with **energy-sector signal simulation**, synthetic data generation for intermittent renewable sources, and demand-side forecasting.

Key methods I worked with daily on my MSc:

| Method | Application Domain |
|---|---|
| Robust L1-ARMA | Periodic energy & climate signals |
| Maximum Entropy Bootstrap (MEBoot) | Synthetic time-series augmentation |
| Bayesian Hierarchical Models + MCMC | Uncertainty quantification in sparse data |
| Spatiotemporal Deep Learning | Remote sensing, satellite multi-source fusion |
| Penalised Regression (Lasso, Ridge, Elastic Net) | High-dimensional feature selection |
| Monte Carlo Simulation | Scenario modelling & stochastic validation |

### Geospatial & Climate AI
At ICS–FORTH I design end-to-end ML pipelines for **climate data ETL, storage, and forecasting** — handling missing observations, irregular sampling, and cross-modal fusion of satellite imagery with ground sensor time-series. The same methodology applies directly to **multi-source energy datasets** (production, consumption, grid state, weather co-variates).
